# Paper Light

~ The screen holds still and keeps the sun,
~ it borrows daylight, spends it slow;
~ a page of ink when day is done
~ still reads by any lamp you know.

~ No glass-bright hurry, no blue glare,
~ no feed that scrolls, no bell that rings —
~ a quiet rectangle of air
~ that only does one thing.

***

# Turning

~ A thumb-click in the winter dark,
~ the panel blinks its slow eclipse;
~ four hundred milliseconds, stark,
~ between two finished sentences.

~ And nothing else. No tab, no chart,
~ no message waiting to be read —
~ the whole appalling modern art
~ of elsewhere, gone instead.

***

# Battery

~ Asleep, it sips a microamp
~ and dreams in charge-retaining cells;
~ a fortnight later, cold and damp,
~ it wakes exactly where it fell —

~ mid-paragraph, mid-thought, mid-line,
~ the bookmark that was never placed,
~ as if no time had passed. No time,
~ so far as it could taste.

***

# Colophon

~ Five keys, one font, eight hundred dots
~ across, four-eighty down the side;
~ a kilobyte of if-and-nots
~ that knows enough to step aside —

~ for that is all a reader's for,
~ whatever else the spec sheet says:
~ to vanish, like a well-hung door,
~ and let the book take place.
