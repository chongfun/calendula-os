# Welcome

This is the Xteink X4 running its open firmware, simulated in your browser. The screen you are reading is drawn by the same Rust code that draws the real 4.26-inch e-paper panel: the same Literata bitmaps, the same line wrap, the same pagination.

Everything here works. Turn pages, open the table of contents, change the type size, put the device to sleep. The panel even flashes the way real e-ink does when it clears ghosting.

Press the Next key (or the right arrow on your keyboard) to turn the page.

# The Five Keys

The X4 has five keys, and the firmware gives each one a single meaning that never changes.

Next and Previous turn pages. In a list, they move the selection instead. They are the two large keys under your right thumb.

Confirm affirms the screen's primary action: it opens the selected book, jumps to the selected chapter, starts a sync. While you are reading, Confirm opens the table of contents.

Back always zooms out one level. Reading to Home, a list to the screen that opened it. You can never get lost: pressing Back enough times always lands on Home.

Power sleeps and wakes the device. Sleep draws a book plate with your current title and progress, then the real hardware powers down to microamps. Try it.

# The Type Menu

Open Settings from Home and you can set the reading type: three sizes, three line spacings, and two weights.

These are not display filters. Changing the size or weight changes where every line wraps, so the firmware repaginates the whole book and keeps your place. The page number may change; your position in the text does not.

The face is Literata, rendered to bitmaps at each size and weight at build time. The panel is one bit deep, so what you see is exactly the ink the firmware placed.

# Wireless

The real device syncs reading progress over Wi-Fi with a KOReader-compatible sync server, and serves a small browser page for uploading books straight to the card.

In this simulation the radio is pretend, but the screens are real: open Sync from Home and press Confirm to watch a session connect, exchange progress, and land back at Done.

That is the whole tour. The other two books on the shelf are better company than this one. Press Back, then open the Library.
